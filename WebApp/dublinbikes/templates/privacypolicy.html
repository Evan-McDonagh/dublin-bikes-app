Privacy Policy Effective date: May 25, 2018 This statement discloses the types of information collected when you visit attackpoint.org and all other web properties or services owned or operated by Attackpoint LLC (collectively, the "Website"), and how we use and safeguard your information. Personal Data Personal data means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. We collect personal data about you: When you visit the Website When you provide it to us directly via the Website When you contact us by email Attackpoint processes your personal data in order to provide you with the services of the Website, and to further our legitimate interests, which include: Providing you with and improving the services of the Website Maintaining the function and security of the Website and your account Providing you support related to your use of the Website Archiving the results of public athletic competitions Personal data collected when you visit the Website When you visit the Website, your browser automatically sends us your IP address and browser version information. We may process this information for the purpose of monitoring the Website for problems and abuse. The legal basis for this processing is our legitimate interest in maintaining the function and security of the Website. Personal data collected when you create an account We process your email and password for the purpose of signing you into your account securely. The legal basis for this processing is our legitimate interest in maintaining the security of your account. We process your email address if you request to reset your account password, or if we need to contact you about some problem with your account. We may also use your email address to associate your account if you contact us for support by email. The legal basis for this processing is our legitimate interest in maintaining the security of your account and providing support. Personal data collected when you use your account You may voluntarily add various types of content under your account (such as your favourited Dublin Bikes stations), which may be considered personal data. The purpose of processing this information is to organize and summarize this information for you. The legal basis for this processing is your consent. You may withdraw your consent at any time by deleting this information from your account. Information sharing Attackpoint may share aggregated anonymized data or statistics for research or commercial purposes. Your private personal data is not shared or sold. It may be disclosed to governmental authorities when legally required to do so, or to a successor entity in connection with a corporate change including the Website. International transfers Attackpoint is operated from Ireland. If you access the Website from outside of the Ireland, you acknowledge that your personal data will be transferred, processed, and stored in Ireland. We have appropriate security measures to guard against the loss, misuse, or alteration of the data we have collected about you. Retention periods We retain the personal data collected from you until you delete the data from your account or you delete your account. Your data will be removed from the Website immediately upon deletion but may remain on backup media for up to 60 days following deletion. In addition, copies of public web pages in third-party archives may remain indefinitely. Your rights If you are located in the EEA, under the General Data Protection Regulation you have the right to request access, rectification, or erasure of your personal data. You have the right to data portability, restriction of processing and objection to processing your personal data. Where we rely on your consent to use your personal data, you may withdraw your consent at any time. You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. Please be aware that we are not responsible for the content or privacy practices of such other sites. We encourage our users to be aware when they leave our site and to read the privacy statements of any other site that collects personal data. Cookies A cookie is a very small text file that is stored on your device, which is used to save information between requests in your browsing session. For example, Attackpoint uses cookies to remember that you have signed in so that you don't have to re-enter your username and password repeatedly while you are using the Website. The Website's use of cookies falls into the following categories: Strictly necessary cookies: These cookies are necessary for the Website to function and cannot be switched off. You can set your browser to block these cookies, but some parts of the site may no longer work and you will not be able to sign into your account Performance cookies: These cookies allow us to monitor visitor traffic and improve the performance of the site. The information these cookies collect is aggregated and anonymous. If you don't allow these cookies, your visits to the site will not be included in our performance analysis. Functional cookies: These cookies allow us to enhance personalization of the site or other optional functionality. If you don't allow these cookies you may not be able to use some of this functionality. The Website does not recognize or respond to browser-initiated Do Not Track signals. Changes If our information practices change at any point in the future, we will update this policy statement. We may also update this statement to improve its clarity. In case of material changes that directly impact your privacy, we will also post a prominent notification or message on the Website.
